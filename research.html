Current Research

Astrometry · 2024

Quasars Proper Motion Dipole

Overview

When the observer is moving, it cause the displacement in the apparent positions of celestial bodies in the direction of the velocity. This effect is called aberration. If the velocity changes with time, which is correspond to the acceleration, the change of displacements create a pattern of proper motions in the direction of acceleration. This effect is called secular aberration drift and can be used to detect the acceleration of the Solar System with respect to the rest frame of distant extragalactic sources.

Theoretically, the proper motion field induced by the acceleration of the Solar System has a unique signature in its power spectrum, which is a pure E-mode dipole pattern. However, measurements of the proper motion field from the Gaia mission have revealed a significant deviation from this expected pattern, with a large B-mode dipole component that is comparable in magnitude to the E-mode dipole. To better understand and quantify these effects, we employ simulation-based inference (SBI) techniques to model the proper motion field and extract constraints on the Solar System's acceleration as well as the power spectrum to identify the systematics.

Publication: Observing the Peculiar Acceleration of our Solar System with Quasar Proper Motions (submitted to A&A)

Spectroscopy · 2024

High-redshift Galaxies

Overview

The history of the Universe is characterized by distinct epochs of structure assembly and formation. Among these, there is a critical period known as the Cosmic Dawn, which marks the time when the first stars and galaxies began to form. This ended the Dark Ages and initiated the Epoch of Reionization, fundamentally transforming the intergalactic medium from neutral to ionized. This is one of the last major phase transitions in the Universe and is suggested that it was largely complete by redshift z ~ 6, which is about 1 billion years after the Big Bang. Therefore, galaxies at these redshifts therefore provide a crucial window into this transitionary era, lying at the boundary between a partially ionized and a fully ionized Universe.
